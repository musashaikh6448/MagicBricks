Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services

Buying a Property

Getting pre-approved involves submitting financial documents to a lender who will review your income, credit score, and debt-to-income ratio. You'll need recent pay stubs, tax returns, bank statements, and employment verification. Pre-approval gives you a clear budget and shows sellers you're a serious buyer.

A thorough home inspection should cover the foundation, roof, electrical systems, plumbing, HVAC, windows, doors, and overall structural integrity. Look for signs of water damage, pest issues, and any safety concerns. Your inspector will provide a detailed report highlighting any issues that need attention.

Your offer should be based on comparable sales in the area, current market conditions, the property's condition, and your budget. In competitive markets, you may need to offer at or above asking price. Your agent will help you develop a competitive offer strategy based on local market data.

Selling a Property

We conduct a comprehensive market analysis comparing your property to similar homes that have recently sold in your area. We consider factors like square footage, lot size, condition, upgrades, and current market trends to determine an optimal listing price that attracts buyers while maximizing your return.

Focus on improvements that offer the best return on investment: fresh paint, updated fixtures, landscaping, and deep cleaning. Kitchen and bathroom updates can be valuable if they're outdated. We'll provide specific recommendations based on your property and local buyer preferences.

The average time varies by market conditions, price range, and property type. In our market area, well-priced homes typically sell within 30-45 days. Luxury properties may take longer due to a smaller buyer pool. Proper pricing and marketing are key factors in reducing time on market.
